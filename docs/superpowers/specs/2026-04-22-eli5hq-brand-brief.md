# ELI5 HQ — Brand Brief

**Status:** Draft for Brand Guardian
**Date:** 2026-04-22
**Owner:** Steve Harmeyer

This document is the strategic brief the Brand Guardian agent will execute
against. It locks the inputs (audience, positioning, voice, editorial formula,
visual direction) and defines the scope of the final Brand Bible deliverable.
It is intentionally prescriptive where strategy is decided, and intentionally
loose where the Brand Guardian is expected to add craft.

---

## 1. Foundations

**Brand name:** ELI5 HQ (spoken: "E-L-I-five H-Q"; full form: ELI5 Headquarters)

**One-line positioning:** The smart-dumb STEM channel — we explain it like
you're 5, but the science is for grown-ups.

**Audience:**

- **Primary:** Gen Z and young adults, roughly 16–24, chronically online,
  STEM-curious but allergic to lectures.
- **Secondary:** Anyone older who enjoys the voice. Designed to *let in* older
  viewers without designing *for* them.

**Content scope:** STEM only — science, tech, math, engineering, AI, plus
adjacent nerd territory (space, biology, chemistry, physics, computing).

**Explicit non-goals:**

- No politics.
- No current-events news cycle.
- No self-help / productivity / life-advice content.
- No talking-head format.
- No trend-chasing dances or lip-syncs.
- No earnest "science is amazing" narration.
- No explainers that insult viewer intelligence.

**Format:** Short-form animated video, 15–60 seconds, published to TikTok,
Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.

**Business model:** Creator channel. Revenue via platform payouts, brand
sponsorships, and affiliate where aligned. Light operations — designed to be
runnable by a solo founder plus a small freelance bench.

**Core tension (the brand's engine):** Pretend-simplicity on the surface, real
depth underneath. "Explain like I'm 5" is the bit. The payoff is that the
viewer finishes smarter than the packaging promised.

## 2. Personality & Voice

**Five personality traits:**

1. **Chaotic-smart** — the kid in class who got the right answer and also drew
   a frog on it.
2. **Irreverent but never mean** — roasts bad science, not people.
3. **Meme-fluent, not meme-dependent** — references land for the terminally
   online without alienating the merely online.
4. **Confidently nerdy** — wears the nerdery loud. No apologizing, no "science
   is cool I promise."
5. **Sharp editor** — every second earns its place. No filler, no
   throat-clearing.

**Voice rules:**

- Short sentences. Then shorter.
- Contractions always. Formal register never.
- Swear-adjacent energy, clean words. ("This is unhinged." "Absolutely feral
  physics.")
- Vocabulary is plain by default; technical terms drop in *with* a translation,
  never as gatekeeping.
- We narrate *to* a friend, not *at* an audience.

**Sound-like examples (✅):**

- *"Okay so imagine your fridge is having a full breakdown. That's basically
  entropy."*
- *"Five-year-old you: 'why is the sky blue?' Physics: 'buckle up.'"*

**Don't-sound-like examples (❌):**

- *"Have you ever wondered why the sky appears blue? Today we'll explore…"*
  (earnest-educational)
- *"POV: you're a photon 😭😭😭"* (overcooked meme voice)

The Brand Guardian should expand this into a full voice/tone spectrum,
vocabulary do/don't list, and at least 20 additional example lines across the
content pillars.

## 3. Editorial Formula

Every video follows this four-beat structure:

1. **Dumb premise hook (0–3s)** — a childlike question or deliberately naive
   framing.
2. **Whiplash pivot (3–8s)** — "actually, here's what's really going on."
3. **Real payoff (8–45s)** — the rigorous explanation, animated, paced for
   retention.
4. **Stinger (last 2–5s)** — a joke, a "wait what," or a callback to the dumb
   premise.

**Content pillars (roughly equal weight):**

- **"Why does X do that?"** — everyday phenomena with weird science underneath.
- **"The math is insane"** — numbers, scale, counterintuitive probability.
- **"Tech, explained dumb"** — AI, chips, networks, how the internet actually
  works.
- **"Nature is unhinged"** — biology, animals, evolution's worst decisions.
- **"Space stuff"** — reliable retention fuel; every channel needs it.

The Brand Guardian should expand each pillar into: topic examples, typical
hook patterns, caption formulas, and visual treatment notes.

## 4. Visual Direction (Guardrails)

These are directional constraints. The Brand Guardian is expected to add
craft — color specifics, type pairings, logo concept, motion principles — but
must stay inside these guardrails.

- **Art style:** flat-vector animation with personality. Cleaner than
  MSPaint-core, looser than Kurzgesagt. Bold shapes, expressive tiny
  characters, chaotic energy inside a tight grid. Must be producible by a solo
  animator or small freelance bench without burning weeks per video.
- **Color:** punchy, ownable primary palette (explicitly *not* Kurzgesagt
  teal, *not* Vox yellow) with a broad secondary set so individual videos can
  feel distinct while still reading as "ours." Direction: saturated, slightly
  off-register, sticker-pack energy.
- **Typography:** two fonts max — one loud display face for hooks/thumbnails,
  one clean sans for captions and body. Must survive at phone-scroll size.
  Open-source or licensed-for-commercial required.
- **Motion:** fast cuts, hard snaps, zero easing-for-easing's-sake. Text pops
  on beat. Nothing lingers.
- **Captions:** burned-in, always, TikTok-native style — but styled as part of
  the brand system, not default CapCut.
- **Logo:** wordmark-led ("ELI5 HQ"), with a compact mark/monogram for avatars
  and intro stings.

## 5. Brand Bible — Deliverable Scope

The Brand Guardian must produce a Brand Bible document containing the
following sections. Each section should be usable by a small team (founder +
freelance animators/editors/designers) to produce consistent output without
re-asking strategic questions.

1. **Brand foundation** — mission, vision, positioning statement, audience
   personas (2–3), competitive map (who we're like, who we're *not* like, why
   we win).
2. **Verbal identity** — voice definition, tone spectrum across pillars,
   vocabulary do/don't list, 20+ example lines, caption formulas, hook
   library (10+ reusable patterns).
3. **Visual identity direction** — logo concept brief (not final art), color
   palette with hex values and usage rules, typography pairing recommendation
   with rationale, art-style mood direction, motion principles.
4. **Editorial system** — four-beat formula expanded with examples, content
   pillar specs, video title/caption patterns, thumbnail rules.
5. **Channel kit** — bio copy for TikTok, Instagram, YouTube; handle
   strategy; profile image direction; pinned-content strategy.
6. **Application guide** — do/don't examples, voice-applied-to-real-topic
   samples across all five pillars, "when in doubt" decision rules.
7. **Governance** — how the brand evolves, what's locked vs flexible, review
   checklist for new videos before publish.

**Explicitly out of scope for this pass:**

- Final logo files (Brand Guardian produces concept brief, not art files).
- Finalized color artwork / swatches as graphics (hex values and usage rules
  are enough).
- Produced video samples or animations.

This bible is the *spec* downstream designers, animators, and editors execute
against. It is not itself the final visual system.

---

## 6. Success Criteria

The Brand Bible succeeds if:

- A freelance animator can read Section 3 (visual) + 4 (editorial) and produce
  a video that feels on-brand on the first attempt.
- A caption writer can read Section 2 (verbal) and produce a hook that passes
  the ✅/❌ test.
- A new channel (e.g., ELI5 HQ on a fourth platform) can be launched using
  Section 5 (channel kit) without re-doing strategy.
- The founder can use Section 7 (governance) to say yes/no to any proposed
  piece of content in under 60 seconds.
