## 1. Brand Foundation

### Mission

Make the hardest ideas in science, tech, and math land in under a minute —
without dumbing them down and without making anyone feel stupid for asking.

### Vision

A generation that treats STEM the way it treats music and memes: as
something you share because it's fun, not because it's homework.

### Positioning Statement

For chronically online 16–24-year-olds who are curious about how the world
works but have zero patience for lectures, **ELI5 HQ** is the short-form
animated STEM channel that explains real science like you're five — and then
actually teaches it. Unlike polished legacy explainer brands (Kurzgesagt,
Vox, SciShow) we're loud, messy, and meme-native. Unlike the TikTok science
creator pool, we hold the line on rigor and a consistent animated identity.

**The engine:** a dumb question on the outside, a rigorous answer on the
inside. The payoff is that you finish smarter than the packaging promised.

### Audience Personas

We build for two primaries and design to let a third ride along.

#### Persona 1 — "Maya, 19, The Bored Honors Kid"

- **Who:** Second-year bio major at a state school. AP-everything background.
  Goes to lectures, comes home, doom-scrolls for three hours.
- **Watches:** Hank Green, Tom Scott reruns, Marques Brownlee when he posts
  shorts, random niche TikTok creators she can't remember the names of.
- **Phone habits:** TikTok 2+ hrs/day, Instagram Reels as a second feed,
  YouTube Shorts while eating. Pays zero attention to the home feed — she
  lives in the algorithm.
- **Why she follows us:** We're smarter than the for-you page average and
  funnier than her professors. We make her feel like her brain isn't wasted.
- **What makes her bounce:** Condescension. Anything that sounds like a
  "hello fellow kids" textbook. Overlong intros. Unfunny.

#### Persona 2 — "Jordan, 17, The Meme Kid Who's Secretly Smart"

- **Who:** High school junior, C+ in chem because it's boring, A+ in
  arguments about whether a hotdog is a sandwich. Learns everything from
  YouTube rabbit holes and comment threads.
- **Watches:** Kurtis Conner, Eddy Burback, meme compilation accounts, the
  occasional Veritasium when YouTube pushes it hard.
- **Phone habits:** TikTok-first. Will never open a YouTube video longer
  than 10 minutes unsolicited. Reads captions with sound off half the time.
- **Why they follow us:** The jokes land. The science sneaks up on them.
  They can quote us at friends.
- **What makes them bounce:** Earnestness. "Science is amazing" narration.
  Anything that feels like a PBS segment. Cringe memes (emoji-screaming, POV
  overuse, outdated slang).

#### Persona 3 — "Alex, 28, The Welcome Hanger-On"

- **Who:** Grad student or early-career engineer. Not our target but not
  allergic to us either. Will share our stuff in group chats.
- **Watches:** Dwarkesh Podcast clips, 3Blue1Brown, whatever their nerdy
  friends post.
- **Why we let them in:** Credibility laundering and share velocity. They're
  the ones who go "wait this is actually right" in replies.
- **What we don't do for them:** Dial back the tone. We are not Alex's
  channel. Alex is a guest.

## 2. Verbal Identity

### Voice Definition

ELI5 HQ talks like **the smartest friend in the group chat who also got sent
to the principal's office in 7th grade.** Rigorous and unserious at the same
time. Fast, loud, specific. Never professorial. Never apologetic about being
nerdy. Never mean.

Three pillars:

1. **Smart-dumb register.** Baby-talk framing, PhD payoff. "Okay so imagine
   a particle is scared of commitment…" → real quantum mechanics.
2. **Tight editorial.** Cut everything that doesn't earn its second. No
   "today we'll explore." No "stay tuned." No "don't forget to like."
3. **Roast the science, never the viewer.** If something's cursed, we say
   it's cursed. If something's counterintuitive, that's physics' fault, not
   yours.

### Tone Spectrum Across the 5 Pillars

Voice is fixed. Tone flexes per pillar:

| Pillar                     | Tone dial                           | Reads like                          | Avoid                           |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Why does X do that?        | Curious-confused, then "oh no"      | A friend who just noticed something | Teacher-voiced wonder           |
| The math is insane         | Deadpan → unhinged                  | A calm person losing it slowly      | "Did you know 🤯" energy        |
| Tech, explained dumb       | Confident, dryly funny              | Someone who works with this stuff   | Tech-bro hype, crypto cadence   |
| Nature is unhinged         | Gossipy, scandalized                | Telling you about a mutual's drama  | Nature-documentary solemnity    |
| Space stuff                | Awe-dampened by sarcasm             | A sigh, then an explanation         | "Space… the final frontier" awe |

### Vocabulary — Do / Don't

**Do use (these are ours):**

- "unhinged," "feral," "cursed," "deranged" — applied to ideas/phenomena
- "okay so," "wait," "actually," "literally," "basically"
- "tiny," "huge," "a lot," "a million," "zero" — plain-number language
- "the math," "the physics," "the chemistry" — treat fields as characters
- "bit," "move," "vibe" — meme-adjacent, low commitment
- Contractions, always. "It's," "that's," "don't," "we're."
- Translations: "entropy (aka mess)," "photon (light particle)."

**Don't use:**

- "folks," "my friends," "hey guys" — host-voiced
- "have you ever wondered" — textbook opener, banned
- "buckle up," "fasten your seatbelts," "strap in" — worn out
- "in this video we'll explore…" — reads as SEO filler
- "mind = blown," "🤯" — cringe by default
- "POV:", "nobody:", "me:" — tired TikTok grammar
- Slurs, punching-down jokes, mean nicknames for groups
- Politics words, even casually
- Emoji spam. One emoji per caption, max, and only if it earns its slot.
- Profanity. We're swear-adjacent clean. "This is unhinged," not the F-word.

### Caption Formulas

Captions are the under-the-video text on TikTok/Reels/Shorts. They work
with the burned-in captions, not against them. Keep them short, scroll-safe,
and built for the algorithm.

**Core structure:** `[hook line or joke] + [one-line reframe] + [3–5
hashtags]`.

- **Dumb-question caption:** `why does [everyday thing] do [weird thing]?
  the real answer is unhinged. #science #eli5 #[pillar]`
- **Stat-bait caption:** `[insane number]. that's it. that's the post.
  #math #eli5 #[topic]`
- **Nature-gossip caption:** `[animal] is [doing cursed thing] and nobody
  talks about it. #biology #eli5 #naturefacts`
- **Tech-flex caption:** `how [tech thing] actually works, explained like
  you're 5 but the answer isn't baby. #tech #ai #eli5`
- **Space-awe caption:** `the universe is [specific unhinged fact] and
  we're just here. #space #astrophysics #eli5`

**Caption rules:**

- Lowercase by default (fits Gen Z platform norms; feels like texting).
- Max 1 emoji. Often zero.
- Always end with 3–5 hashtags: one brand (`#eli5hq` or `#eli5`), one pillar
  (`#math`, `#space`, `#biology`, `#tech`, `#physics`), one topic-specific.
- No "link in bio" in early content. Earn that later.

### Hook Library — 12 Reusable Patterns

Every video must open with a pattern-matched hook in the first 2 seconds.
These are fill-in-the-blanks. Mix them; don't run the same one twice in a
week.

1. **Dumb-question:** `why does [thing] [do thing]?`
   → _"why does the sky go orange at sunset?"_
2. **Five-year-old framing:** `okay imagine you're 5 and you asked [dumb
   question]. here's what's actually happening.`
   → _"okay imagine you're 5 and you asked why ice floats. buckle—nope."_
3. **Wait-what:** `wait. [counterintuitive fact]. actually wait.`
   → _"wait. you can't touch anything. actually wait."_
4. **Numeric gut-punch:** `there are more [x] than [y]. let that cook.`
   → _"there are more stars than grains of sand. let that cook."_
5. **Accusation:** `[field of science] is lying to you and here's how.`
   → _"chemistry is lying to you and here's how."_
6. **Cursed fact drop:** `[insane true thing]. that's real. that's happening
   right now.`
   → _"there's a cloud of alcohol in space. that's real."_
7. **Everyday-to-cosmic:** `you do [normal thing]. physics does [insane
   thing] so that works.`
   → _"you open the fridge. physics does interpretive dance so that works."_
8. **Roast setup:** `[animal/phenomenon] is the [unhinged superlative] and
   nobody's talking about it.`
   → _"the mantis shrimp is the worst roommate in the ocean."_
9. **Wrong-on-purpose:** `[confidently wrong statement]. ok not really.
   here's the real answer.`
   → _"the sun is on fire. ok not really. it's worse."_
10. **Class-failed:** `they told you [school version]. they were lying for
    convenience. the truth is—`
    → _"they told you atoms are balls with dots. they lied."_
11. **Receipts:** `i will now prove [absurd claim] in under 30 seconds.`
    → _"i will now prove you're mostly empty space in under 30 seconds."_
12. **Name-the-villain:** `[scientific concept], explained. it's the reason
    [annoying everyday thing].`
    → _"entropy, explained. it's the reason your room is messy."_

## 3. Visual Identity Direction

### Logo Concept Brief

**Name lockup:** `ELI5 HQ` — wordmark-led, not icon-led.

**Concept direction for the designer:**

- **Primary lockup (wordmark):** The characters `ELI5 HQ` set tight and
  confident. The `5` should do the heavy lifting — it's the personality
  character. Treatments worth exploring:
  - The `5` drawn as a hand-scrawled / sticker-style digit — deliberately a
    little off, like it was drawn by a smart kid, against a tight geometric
    `ELI` and `HQ`.
  - Or: `5` as a comic-style balloon with a tiny highlight, pasted onto the
    wordmark like a sticker on a laptop.
- **Monogram / avatar mark:** A square-crop version featuring only the `5`
  (as developed above) on a solid Hazard Pink or Off-Black tile. This is
  what lives in profile pictures and intro stings. It must read at 40×40px.
- **Secondary mark (optional):** `HQ` in a small bracket/stamp treatment
  (like a government-surplus ink stamp) for use as an end-card signature.
  Optional, not load-bearing.
- **Energy check:** Confident but hand-touched. Not corporate. Not cutesy.
  Think: sticker slapped on a textbook by somebody who aced the class.

**Hard rules for the designer:**

- No gradients in the primary lockup. Flat only.
- The `5` must be visually distinct enough to work alone as the avatar.
- Works in single-color knockout (white on color, color on off-black).
- Minimum wordmark width: 120px digital / 30mm print.
- Clear space around lockup: the height of the `5` on all sides.
- **Don't:** science-cliché iconography (atoms, beakers, gears, lightbulbs,
  brain outlines). We are not stock.

### Logo — Construction Rules (Locked as of v1.1)

The primary wordmark is a **three-part lockup** with a fixed color role
for every part. Deviation is a rebrand.

**Parts:**

1. **`ELI`** — set in Space Grotesk 700, tight (-4% tracking), in
   **Chalk White** (`#FAFAF5`) on dark surfaces or **Off-Black** (`#0E0E12`)
   on light surfaces. The cream/white does the weight.
2. **Hazard-pink panel hugging the `5`** — a solid Hazard Pink (`#FF2E63`)
   rectangle rotated **-2°**, with the `5` **knocked out to the surface
   color** (i.e. the `5` is transparent / same color as whatever sits
   behind the lockup). This is the load-bearing personality moment.
3. **`HQ` stamp** — a Chalk Yellow (`#F5E663`) rectangle with Off-Black
   `HQ` set in Space Grotesk 700, tracked +20%, sitting at the cap-height
   line. Third color, small. Never pink. Never cream.

**Rules:**

- The `5` is **always** knocked out to the surface — you must pass the
  correct background color when you render the mark. On photos or
  gradients, place the lockup over a solid color card first.
- **Color roles are fixed.** ELI=cream/black (depending on surface) ·
  panel=Hazard Pink · 5=surface · HQ stamp=Chalk Yellow. Swapping any
  role is off-brand.
- **Rotation is fixed.** Panel tilts -2°. HQ stamp sits flat.
- **HQ is a stamp, not a co-star.** Its cap-height is ~14% of the ELI
  cap-height. Don't resize it up for balance.
- **Minimum legibility size:** at cap-heights below ~14px the HQ stamp
  stops reading cleanly — below that, use the **Monogram** alone.

**Avatar / monogram variants (all use the same knockout construction):**

| Variant | Tile | 5 | Use |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `pink` (primary) | Hazard Pink | surface color (knockout) | Default avatar everywhere |
| `black` (reserved) | Off-Black | Hazard Pink | Pinned stories, temporary events only |
| `cream` (secondary) | Paper Cream | Hazard Pink | Podcast covers, warm-ground contexts |

**Canonical assets live in `/assets`:**

- `logo-wordmark.svg` — full lockup
- `monogram-5-pink.svg` — primary avatar
- `monogram-5-black.svg` — reserved variant

### Color Palette

We own a palette that is emphatically **not teal and not yellow**. The
direction is saturated, slightly acidic, sticker-pack. One loud primary,
one grounded anchor, a controlled chaos secondary set.

#### Primary

| Role            | Name          | Hex       | Use                                                          |
| --------------- | ------------- | --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Brand primary   | Hazard Pink   | `#FF2E63` | The main brand color. Logo, key hooks, button-equivalents, accent across every video. Loud, ownable, not used by our competitors. |
| Brand anchor    | Off-Black     | `#0E0E12` | Dominant background. Not pure `#000` — softer, printable, feels designed. |
| Brand light     | Paper Cream   | `#F5F1E8` | Alternate background for "daylight" videos. Not white. Warm, slightly aged, feels like a sticker sheet. |

**Why this combo wins:** Hazard Pink is absent from Kurzgesagt, Vox,
SciShow, Veritasium, MinuteEarth. It reads instantly as ours on the for-you
page. Off-Black gives us cinematic weight without corporate tech-black.
Paper Cream keeps us warm — we're not a terminal, we're a sticker book.

#### Usage Rules

- **60/30/10:** ~60% Off-Black or Paper Cream (ground), ~30% one secondary
  (topic color), ~10% Hazard Pink (brand signature).
- **Hazard Pink is not optional.** Every video has pink somewhere — a
  caption highlight, a mark, a callout. It's how the channel reads as ours.
- **Never pair Hazard Pink with Signal Orange.** They fight.
- **Captions:** Chalk White text with a Hazard Pink or Lab Lime keyword
  highlight on the *one* word in the line that has to land.
- **Thumbnails:** Off-Black background, one secondary accent, Hazard Pink
  on the hook word. Face-sized emoji/characters, big type.
- **Accessibility:** Body text pairings must hit WCAG AA (4.5:1). Our
  defaults pass: Chalk White on Off-Black (17:1), Off-Black on Paper Cream
  (13:1), Off-Black on Hazard Pink (5.1:1). **Do not** put Chalk White on
  Hazard Pink for body text — fails at 3.3:1. Use it only on short hooks
  ≥ 32pt where it passes as large-text AA.

### Typography

Two fonts, no more. Both open-source, both free for commercial use, both
survive the phone scroll.

#### Display — **Space Grotesk** (700 / 500)

- **Why:** Wide, confident, slightly geometric but with enough humanist
  warmth to not feel corporate-SaaS. Has a personality `5` with character
  (works with our logo concept). Open-source (Google Fonts / SIL OFL).
- **Use for:** Hooks, thumbnail type, on-screen key words, title cards,
  stingers. Bold 700 default, Medium 500 for support.
- **Rules:** Can be set tight (-2 to -4% tracking) for hooks. Never set
  below 24pt on video. Never italicize.

#### Text — **Inter** (600 / 500 / 400)

- **Why:** The most-battle-tested UI sans font on Earth. Perfect
  legibility at small sizes, huge character set, every weight we need.
  Open-source (SIL OFL). Pairs cleanly with Space Grotesk (similar x-height,
  different personality — geometric display + neutral body is textbook
  pairing).
- **Use for:** Burned-in captions, body text on graphics, social bio copy,
  channel description, anything long-form.
- **Rules:** 600 for captions (phone legibility), 500 for supporting lines,
  400 for small UI/legal only. Sentence case always except where a line is
  a single hook word.

**Pairing defensible alternatives (if Space Grotesk is unavailable):**
Display substitute = **Archivo Black** or **Bagel Fat One**. Body substitute
= **Manrope** or **DM Sans**. Do not mix more than one display + one text.

### Frame Anatomy

Every video frame is a 9:16 (1080×1920) canvas with three locked zones:

- **Top 14% — chrome safe zone.** Holds the wordmark on Beats 1–3 (cream/black `ELI` + hazard-pink panel with knockout `5` + chalk-yellow `HQ` stamp, ~14px font-size). No caption content crosses into this band.
- **Center 64% — caption region.** Holds all sticker pills. Each pill rotates between −2° and +2°, picked deterministically from a hash of the pill's text so the same line always tilts the same way across re-renders.
- **Bottom 22% — platform UI safe zone.** Reserved for usernames, CTAs, and caption trays. Empty on Beats 1–3. Beat 4 hosts the bottom-dominant wordmark + small `@eli5hq` handle here.

**Hazard Pink invariant.** Every video must include at least one Hazard
Pink caption pill. If pill-color assignment doesn't naturally produce one,
the renderer forces pink onto Beat 2's first pill (the pivot reads loudest
in pink).

### Art-Style Mood Direction

- **Flat-vector with personality.** Hard shapes. Visible "drawn by a human"
  quirk in line weights and character faces. Imperfect circles. Slightly
  off-register fills (shapes a few pixels off their outlines) for sticker
  energy.
- **Tiny expressive characters.** When we anthropomorphize a concept
  (e.g., an atom, a photon, a neutron star), it's a little blob with
  eyes and one expressive feature (shaky legs, a spike, a sweatbead).
  No faces on actual humans — humans are silhouettes or hands only. Human
  faces risk aging the brand fast.
- **Chaotic energy inside a tight grid.** Backgrounds are clean flat
  color. Foregrounds can explode with sticker-style labels, arrows, hand-
  drawn circles, scribbled underlines. The grid holds; the stickers break
  it.
- **Not Kurzgesagt:** no cinematic gradients, no soft glows, no planet-size
  tableaus, no silk-smooth easing.
- **Not Vox:** no journalistic charts, no photo cutouts, no serif
  credibility moves.
- **Reference points (mood, not copy):** packaging stickers, zines, 90s
  educational posters, the Everything Everywhere All At Once title cards,
  Adam J Kurtz's lettering energy, the density of a _Oh No Ross and Carrie_
  merch table.

### Motion Principles

- **Cut on the word.** Motion matches the voice: fast, punchy, on-beat.
- **No easing for easing's sake.** Snap in, snap out. If something needs
  to drift, it's because the joke requires it.
- **Text is a character.** Type scales, rotates, slams. Every key word
  gets its own entrance.
- **Frame holds max 1.2s.** If a frame doesn't change visually within a
  beat, we recut.
- **Zoom > pan.** Punch-ins and whip cuts over slow pans.
- **Sound design is part of motion.** Snaps, pops, one unhinged foley hit
  per stinger. (Handled by editor; animator should leave room.)
- **No talking head, ever.** If a human appears, it's a silhouette or a
  hand drawing on a chalkboard — never a face.

#### Cue Verb Vocabulary

The motion principles above ("type slams, rotates, scales", "every key word
gets its own entrance", "snap in, snap out") cash out as eight named cue
verbs that compositions can implement and prompts can teach:

- **slam** — word punches in: scale 1.4→1.0, ±3° rotation overshoot, 1px land-shake. Use for ALL-CAPS hook words and pivot punches.
- **scale-bounce** — word grows past target then settles (1.0→1.25→1.0). Use for question marks and giant numbers that "refuse to fit the frame."
- **zoom-punch** — whole-frame brief zoom (1.0→1.05→1.0) anchored on a word. Use for sentence-final reveals in Beat 3.
- **circle-mark** — hand-drawn hazard-pink circle traces around a word. Use to single out a real term (e.g., *cavitation*) in the payoff.
- **underline-scribble** — wobbly hazard-pink underline draws beneath a word. Use for italic-emphasis equivalents.
- **x-out** — pink X slams over a word, word fades to 50%. Use for the "X'd out" rejection pattern (e.g., "is it grandma? ✗").
- **sticker-drop** — small sticker (arrow / exclamation / question) drops next to a word. Use sparingly; one per video max.
- **whip-cut** — pill-level entrance with motion-blur, no scale or rotate. Use for the first pill of Beat 2 — the structural pivot.

Implementations may auto-derive most verbs from text content (ALL-CAPS,
trailing punctuation, italic markdown). Hand-authored cues are reserved for
intent that the text alone can't express — circle-mark, sticker-drop.

## 4. Editorial System

### The Four-Beat Formula (Expanded)

Every video hits every beat. No exceptions.

| Beat                | Timing      | Job                                                                      | Fail state                                  |
| ------------------- | ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------- |
| **1. Dumb premise** | 0–3s        | Stop the thumb. Ask the dumbest honest version of the question.          | Earnest intro, "today we'll explore…"       |
| **2. Whiplash pivot** | 3–8s      | "Actually—" snap into the real topic. Signal: we're not joking anymore.  | Joke keeps going. Science never shows up.   |
| **3. Real payoff**  | 8–45s       | The actual explanation. Rigorous. Translated, not dumbed down. Animated. | Hand-wavy. Vague. "Basically it's quantum." |
| **4. Stinger**      | last 2–5s   | Callback joke, wait-what, or absurdist bow. Shareable moment.            | Earnest thank-you. "Follow for more."       |

**Overall video length targets:** 22–45s is the sweet spot. 15s for
single-fact drops. 60s is the soft target — aim here. 90s is the hard
ceiling (Instagram Reels' cap) — if it needs more than 90s, it's a
two-parter.

### Per-Beat Background Matrix

The four beats render against a fixed 5×4 lookup table — pillar × beat
index. Beat 1 reflects the pillar's preferred warm/dark BG; Beat 2 cuts to
dark to register the whiplash pivot; Beats 3–4 share the pillar accent
full-bleed (no internal cut between them — Beat 4 is structurally a
sub-beat of the payoff).

| Pillar  | Beat 1        | Beat 2     | Beat 3            | Beat 4            |
|---------|---------------|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| why     | Paper Cream   | Off-Black  | Signal Orange     | Signal Orange     |
| math    | Off-Black     | Smoke 900  | Lab Lime          | Lab Lime          |
| tech    | Off-Black     | Smoke 900  | Beaker Blue       | Beaker Blue       |
| nature  | Paper Cream   | Off-Black  | Moss Green        | Moss Green        |
| space   | Off-Black     | Smoke 900  | Plasma Violet     | Plasma Violet     |

The pillar deep-spec accent colors stay as caption-pill text colors and
sticker-decoration colors; only background assignment is governed by this
matrix.

#### Beat 4 Stinger Frame

The stinger frame breaks the chrome convention from Beats 1–3 to mark the
shareable moment:

- Background continues from Beat 3 — same pillar accent, no cut.
- Wordmark relocates from top to bottom (centered, ~28px, ~2× the chrome scale). The handle `@eli5hq` appears in small caps above it.
- Caption pills stack vertically in the center band, sized larger than other beats. Every word auto-slams; the final word auto-scale-bounces.
- Optional **stamp** — a chalk-yellow rubber-stamp sticker rotated −8°, ≤32 chars, positioned bottom-right of the caption region. The three sample stinger scripts in §4 all exhibit this pattern: "OUT OF ORDER", "5/5 NO NOTES", "FIRST SHUFFLER OF THIS EXACT DECK — 2026."
- **"Follow for more" remains the documented fail state** — the stinger is a callback joke or absurdist bow, not an earnest CTA.

### Video Title Patterns

Titles work across all three platforms (caption on TikTok/Reels, title on
YouTube Shorts). Keep under 60 characters.

- `why does [X]`
- `[thing] is [unhinged superlative]`
- `[stat] and nobody's talking about it`
- `[field] is lying to you`
- `explaining [concept] in [time]`
- `[animal/concept] PSA`
- `the [concept] situation`

### Thumbnail Rules

For YouTube Shorts (the only surface where a true custom thumbnail matters):

1. **Off-Black background.** Always. Never Paper Cream for thumbnails —
   contrast on mobile beats warmth.
2. **Three elements max:** one character/object, one hook phrase (≤ 4
   words, Space Grotesk 700), one Hazard Pink accent (a circle, underline,
   or the character's outline).
3. **Hook word highlighted** in Hazard Pink or Lab Lime. One word. Never
   two. That's the thumb-stopper.
4. **Never** use:
   - Shocked-face human photos
   - Red arrows
   - The word "SHOCKING" or similar bait
   - Emojis over 32pt
   - Gradients
5. **Type ≥ 80pt** at 1080×1920 export. Assume phone preview.
6. **Contrast test:** convert to grayscale in your head. If the hook word
   doesn't survive, redo it.

## 6. Application Guide

### Do / Don't Examples (Side-by-Side)

**On a hook:**

- ✅ *"why does your knuckle crack?"*
- ❌ *"Have you ever wondered what makes that popping sound when you crack
  your knuckles?"*

**On a stat:**

- ✅ *"there are more shuffles than atoms on earth. let that cook."*
- ❌ *"Did you know… 52 factorial is a truly mind-boggling number! 🤯"*

**On a stinger:**

- ✅ *"anyway. your brain is a three-pound electric meat sponge. sleep
  well."*
- ❌ *"thanks for watching! don't forget to like and subscribe!"*

**On a title:**

- ✅ *"platypus PSA"*
- ❌ *"TOP 5 WEIRD FACTS ABOUT PLATYPUSES YOU WON'T BELIEVE!!!"*

**On a caption:**

- ✅ *"why does rain smell good? dirt bacteria having a party. #science
  #eli5 #weather"*
- ❌ *"🌧️✨ Have you ever noticed the beautiful smell of rain?? 🌧️✨ Let us
  know in the comments!!! 💬"*

**On visuals:**

- ✅ A little blob-shaped photon with sweatbeads zooming across a
  Plasma-Violet background, label slamming on in Space Grotesk 700.
- ❌ A slow, gradient-soft tableau of planets with cinematic easing.

### Voice-Applied-to-a-Real-Topic

See Section 4 for three fully-written sample scripts — knuckle cracking
(Why does X do that?), deck shuffling (The math is insane), and the
platypus PSA (Nature is unhinged). Those are the canonical voice
demonstrations. When writing for Tech or Space, model the beat pacing
and tonal shifts from those three; the tone-spectrum table in Section 2
specifies how voice flexes per pillar.

### "When in Doubt" Decision Rules

Print these. Tape them to the monitor.

1. **If the hook starts with "have you ever wondered" — rewrite.**
2. **If there's no real science by second 8 — cut to it.**
3. **If the word "basically" is doing all the work — you haven't explained it.**
4. **If it could live on a kids' channel or a news channel — it's not ours.**
5. **If you haven't used Hazard Pink on-screen — add it.**
6. **If the joke punches down at the viewer — kill the joke, keep the science.**
7. **If a talking head appears — stop. We don't do that.**
8. **If a frame holds longer than 1.2s — recut or add motion.**
9. **If the stinger is a thank-you — replace with a callback.**
10. **If you're not sure it's on-brand — read it aloud. Does it sound like
    the group chat?**

## 7. Governance

### What's Locked vs Flexible

**Locked** (changing these requires a founder-signed brief revision):

- Audience (Gen Z 16–24 primary)
- STEM-only scope + the no-politics / no-news / no-self-help walls
- The five content pillars
- The four-beat formula
- No-talking-head rule
- Brand primary color (Hazard Pink `#FF2E63`) + anchor (Off-Black `#0E0E12`)
- **Chalk Yellow `#F5E663`** — reserved for the logo HQ stamp only. Not a pillar color.
- Typography pair (Space Grotesk + Inter)
- The name "ELI5 HQ" and the logo's load-bearing `5`
- **Logo construction rules (v1.1):** three-part lockup · cream/black ELI · Hazard-Pink panel with knockout 5 · Chalk-Yellow HQ stamp · panel tilts -2°. See §3 "Logo — Construction Rules."

**Flexible** (the team can iterate without a brief revision):

- Secondary color palette (can add a sixth sticker-pack color; remove only
  with founder sign-off)
- Caption formulas (evolve based on what the algorithm rewards)
- Hook library (add new patterns as they emerge; retire tired ones)
- Motion-design specifics (new transitions, new foley kit)
- Posting cadence, platform mix, thumbnail experiments
- Bio copy (A/B test freely)
- Which sticker-pack color maps to which pillar (if data shows better pairings)

### How the Brand Evolves

- **Quarterly review.** Founder + any active freelancers. 30 minutes.
  Look at: which pillar is over/underperforming, which visual moves landed,
  which voice patterns got reused, any "when in doubt" rule that got broken
  successfully (new rule candidate).
- **Annual refresh.** Revisit palette, hook library, persona profiles.
  Retire the intro short. Maybe add a pillar. Probably don't remove one.
- **No surprise rebrands.** The `5`, Hazard Pink, Space Grotesk, and the
  four-beat formula stay put for at least 24 months from launch. Channel
  memory is the moat.

### Pre-Publish Review Checklist (60-Second Run)

Before the founder approves any video to publish, run this list. If any
answer is "no," don't ship.

1. ☐ Does the hook land in the first 2 seconds with no "today we'll" energy?
2. ☐ Is there a clear whiplash pivot by second 8?
3. ☐ Is the actual science accurate, with a source you could point to if asked?
4. ☐ Is every technical term translated in plain language on first use?
5. ☐ Is the video between 15 and 90 seconds (target ≤ 60s)?
6. ☐ Does Hazard Pink appear on screen at least once?
7. ☐ Are captions burned in, styled in our type (Inter 600+), not CapCut default?
8. ☐ Is there a stinger — a callback, joke, or "wait what" — in the last 2–5s?
9. ☐ Zero: talking heads, politics, current-events, self-help, punching-down?
10. ☐ Does the voiceover use contractions and short sentences throughout?
11. ☐ Is the thumbnail (Shorts only) Off-Black BG + 1 accent + ≤ 4-word hook?
12. ☐ Is the caption under 200 chars with 3–5 hashtags, one of which is the pillar?
13. ☐ Does the title fit one of our title patterns?
14. ☐ Read the script aloud: does it sound like a smart friend, or a teacher?
15. ☐ If someone shared this with a friend, is there a line they'd quote?

If all 15 pass: ship it.
